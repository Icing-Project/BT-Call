## Key Design Decisions

### Why These Parameters?

**Sample Rate: 16 kHz**
- Voice band: 300-3400 Hz fits comfortably
- Nyquist: 8 kHz allows frequencies up to ~3.5 kHz
- Higher quality than 8 kHz, lower data than 48 kHz

**Symbol Rate: 100 baud**
- 2 bits/symbol = 200 bps raw throughput
- Allows ~2.8 kbps after framing (enough for voice + overhead)
- Slow enough for robust detection on noisy channels

**Frequencies: [600, 900, 1200, 1500] Hz**
- All within phone band (300-3400 Hz)
- 300 Hz spacing minimizes crosstalk
- Avoids DTMF tones (697-1633 Hz) mostly

**Codec2 Mode 1400**
- Good balance: 1.4 kbps voice quality
- Robust to noise
- Lower latency than 700C mode

**FEC RS(255,223)**
- Can correct up to 16 symbol errors
- ~12% overhead (32 parity bytes)
- Standard RS configuration
